The Anatomy of a Great Work

Three different projects — a temple, a wall, and a people's own hearts — all move through the very same five stages.

"I am doing a great work, so that I cannot come down." — Nehemiah 6:3

The Temple
Zerubbabel & the exiles · Ezra 1; 3–6
The CallEzra 1:1–3

The Lord stirs up Cyrus; the exiles are sent home to build the house of the Lord.

Starting the WorkEzra 3:8–11

They build the altar, then lay the temple's foundation with trumpets and singing.

OppositionEzra 4:1–5, 24

Adversaries "weaken their hands," hire counselors, and halt the work by letters to the king.

Pressing ThroughEzra 5:1–2

Haggai and Zechariah stir them; Zerubbabel rises and builds, appealing to Cyrus's decree.

CompletionEzra 6:15–16, 22

The temple is finished (516 BC) and dedicated with a joyful Passover.

The Wall
Nehemiah · Nehemiah 2; 4–6
The CallNehemiah 2:5–8

The cupbearer is sent to rebuild Jerusalem's wall — "the good hand of my God upon me."

Starting the WorkNehemiah 2:17–18

He inspects the ruins by night, then rallies the people: "Let us rise up and build."

OppositionNehemiah 4:1–3; 6:1–4

Sanballat mocks and plots; four times he tries to lure Nehemiah "down" to a trap.

Pressing ThroughNehemiah 4:16–20; 6:3

They build with sword and trowel: "I am doing a great work… I cannot come down."

CompletionNehemiah 6:15–16

The wall is finished in 52 days; even enemies see it "was wrought of our God."

The People
Ezra & the Water Gate · Ezra 7; Nehemiah 8
The CallEzra 7:6, 10

Ezra "prepared his heart to seek… do… and teach" the law, and the king sends him.

Starting the WorkNehemiah 8:1–5

The whole people gather at the Water Gate and the book of the law is opened before all.

OppositionNehemiah 8:9

Seeing how far they had drifted, the people are overcome — grief threatens to stall the day.

Pressing ThroughNehemiah 8:8, 10

The word is read and explained; "the joy of the Lord is your strength" turns tears to gladness.

CompletionNehemiah 8:17

They keep the Feast of Tabernacles with "very great gladness" and renew the covenant.
